Big pay, big difference

The gap between the top paid college football coach and the 100th is vast. But how much does pay = wins?

By Adam Furley

Wins do not closely correlate to how much college football head coaches earn in their salary each season, according to an analysis of data. There will always be outliers around the middle. For example, Ohio State's Urban Meyer, Michigan's Jim Harbaugh and Alabama's Nick Saban all make the most money in college football and they are all proven winners. Nebraska’s Mike Riley, on the other hand, is right in the middle. Making the average amount of money but is at about 8 wins per season.

Harbaugh is making the most in college football by earning a little more than $9 million per season. He’s averaging 10 wins per season at Michigan. The 100th ranked coach by pay is South Alabama’s Joey Jones. He is making a little over $300,000 a season. Over a season, Harbaugh's single-game paycheck is more than twice as much as Jones' whole season pay.

There are a few coaches on this list that no longer coach for that school. For example, Charlie Strong and Mark Helfrich no longer coach at Texas and Oregon. This graph shows you that no matter how much money you make, if you don’t win, you won’t keep your job.
